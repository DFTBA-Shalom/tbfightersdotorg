US Government Freezes Important TB Funding

Contact Your Representatives Now

The Freeze

In the first weeks of the Trump presidency, the administration issued an executive order halting all foreign aid. This includes USAID funding, which is crucial to the fight against TB. USAID funding allows people across the world to get the needed medicine and tests for a plethora of diseases, including TB.


The order also freezes funding for an important HIV/AIDS treatment & prevention program called the President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief, or PEPFAR. The suspension has stopped any testing for HIV and distribution of HIV drugs under the program. PEPFAR is necessary to treat and reduce HIV across the world.

The Impact

The suspension of funding means that tuberculosis treatments will be stopped prematurely. This makes it more likely that drug-resistant TB will develop and spread widely, which in turn makes it harder to treat. The funding freeze will also cause many people to die because they do not get the necessary testing and drugs to treat their case.


The stoppage of PEPFAR will have huge impacts on the treatment of TB and HIV globally too. To date, PEPFAR has saved the lives of over 26 million people. HIV and TB form a lethal combination, each speeding the other's progress. Individuals with HIV are 16 times more likely to fall ill with tuberculosis, and TB is the leading cause of death in people with HIV.


These actions will be devastating for the fight against TB, HIV and other diseases globally.
